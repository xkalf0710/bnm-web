Buir Lake

Location

Buir Lake, located in the eastern part of Mongolia along the border with China, is one of the country’s largest and most beautiful freshwater lakes. Covering an area of around 610 square kilometers, this pristine lake is known for its clear waters and stunning natural surroundings. The lake is fed by the Khalkh River and is home to a diverse range of fish, making it a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts. The area around Buir Lake is characterized by rolling grasslands and a rich variety of wildlife, including waterfowl and other bird species, making it an excellent spot for birdwatching. The lake is also significant for its proximity to historical sites, as the surrounding region has witnessed key events during Mongolia's ancient and medieval history, particularly during the time of the Mongol Empire...

 Get Direction

...Visitors to Buir Lake can enjoy the serene environment, camp by its shores, and explore the peaceful landscapes that stretch into the horizon. The lake is a perfect retreat for those seeking natural beauty, outdoor activities, and a connection with Mongolia’s vast steppe and water ecosystems.
