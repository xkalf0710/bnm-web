Khogno Tarna NP

Location

Khogno Tarna National Park is a remarkable destination where Mongolia’s diverse landscapes meet. Located about 280 km west of Ulaanbaatar, this park is often referred to as the “Mini Gobi” because of the unique combination of sand dunes, mountain ranges, and lush green valleys that exist together in one location. Visitors can explore the park’s contrasting landscapes by hiking, camel riding, and visiting historic monasteries.

Elsen Tasarkhai

The Elsen Tasarkhai dunes are the highlight of Khogno Tarna National Park, stretching for about 80 km across the steppe. These golden sand dunes are often compared to the dunes of the Gobi Desert, but what makes them special is their proximity to green pastures and rocky mountains, creating a dramatic contrast in the landscape. The name “Elsen Tasarkhai” translates to “a piece of sand,” and the dunes live up to their name, appearing as a sandy island in the middle of Mongolia’s steppe. You can take a camel ride across the dunes, which is a must-do experience, especially if you’ve never ridden a camel before. The dunes are also perfect for hiking and photography, especially in the soft light of the early morning or late afternoon. If you’re feeling adventurous, some visitors try sandboarding here, though it’s more of a fun challenge than an organized activity.

Mountain Range

The Khogno Khan Mountain Range offers a completely different experience from the nearby sand dunes. These ancient, rugged mountains are great for hiking and wildlife spotting. The trails around the mountains lead you through rocky terrain, with stunning views of the surrounding steppe and valleys. One of the great things about Khogno Khan is its rich spiritual history. Hidden in the foothills of the mountains, you’ll find the ruins of Erdene Khamba Monastery, which offers a quiet, reflective experience for visitors. Hiking in this region also gives you the chance to encounter some of Mongolia’s unique wildlife, like ibex, argali sheep, and sometimes even eagles soaring above the cliffs. The mountain is not only a natural wonder but also a spiritual place, making it a favorite for both adventure seekers and those interested in the history of Mongolian Buddhism.
